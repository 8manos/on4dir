THE ORIGIN OF THE ORIGIN

In 2015 4Direcciones was commissioned by the Museum of Art of the National University of Colombia to carry out a series of large-format works on our relationship and dialogue with indigenous communities. The curator, María Belén Saez de Ibarra, had explored the relationship between the contemporary world and indigenous knowledge in the exhibition "Cosmopolitical Jungle" and she had seen up close 4Direcciones passion for indigenous issues in Colombia. She asked us to review all our work and based on researching our own and external archives, to create a new piece of art that could create in the spectator a real transformation, analogous to the one that happens when taking sacred plants in ceremonies. A challenge at all levels. Thus we came to the conclusion that a transforming aspect of the ceremonies derives from the sound environment.

The vibration from the sound renews the information from the water in the blood and allows several levels of sensory transformation. We worked for 12 months researching sound files and doing studio recordings of specific songs with traditional authorities from the Amazon and also from Kogui and Wayuu villages. We worked collectively with a large group of elders and with Miguel Navas the composer with whom we created the centerpiece of The Origin of Night; An immersive sound installation in a space of 800 mts2, 16 channels and 22 sound booths.

What we did was a creation of a Maloca (longhouse) constructed by sound where an audio ceremony of 2 hours and 30 minutes took place. The exhibition opened on September 13, 2016 and was open until April 27, 2017, receiving an average of 600 visitors a day. Many of them were a captive audience, spectators and students who took refuge in the dark room for rest, renewal, kissing or simply to sleep and have revealing dreams for two semesters.

BIO

DIANA RICO

Diana Rico Bogotá, April 4th 1970.

Film Director, writer and visual artist from the Universidad de los Andes, Diana has a long track record in the field of the arts, cultural management, cinema journalism and critic for several written and radio media in Colombia. Creator and director of several TV programs including “Cinearte” that aired between 1999-2012 at Canal Caracol TV . In 2007 she founded, along with Richard Decaillet, 4Direcciones Audiovisual a production company, internationally awarded for its environmental, indigenous, cultural and for children content for cinema, TV, mobile devices and Web. The experience of 4 Direcciones includes shorts, feature films , series and animations that reach audiences in Latam, Canada, United States, Asia and Europe, art installations and Music Festivals. Since 2007 is an ally in the audiovisual processes of GAIA Amazonas www.gaiaamazonas.org . Along with Peter Webber (Oscar nominee for Girl with a Pearl Earning) she co-directed and wrote The Sand and The Rain a documentary series for Qatar and in 2012 won the MipJunior Competition in Cannes for one of the animated series she has written; Omm Moo Yoga for kids, presently airing in over 15 territories, including India, USA and Portugal. She has been also a producer for series such as Light at the Edge of the World by Wade Davis for Natgeo ( 2010) Narcos for Netflix,(2014-2106) Animal Planet Discovery Channel and BBC. She is Executive Producer and Associate Director with Peter Webber and Richard Decaillet of PICKPOCKETS to be released by NETFLIX end of 2017. As a Visual Artist was commissioned with Richard Decaillet by the Museum of Art of the National University of Colombia to craft the large format work “The Origin of the Night”, (2016) an immersive sound installation and by the Museum of Modern Art of Medellin, “Cuca:The Multinaturalists” (2017). Since 1999 Diana began a thorough study of native traditions of the Americas and established a vital commitment with the care and guardianship of the altars and songs of ancient ceremonies. She is an official singer of Sun Dance and GhostDance in Ecuador/Mexico/Spain and Colombia.

RICHARD DECAILLET

Richard Décaillet Quintana , Bogota 1975.

Richard is an artist, audiovisual director and producer graduated from the National School of Fine Arts, of Paris, France. With a background in photography, video art and experimental cinema from the School of Art Institute of Chicago,USA and the School of Visual Arts of geneva, Switzerland. He has exhibited his artistic work in galleries and institutions of several countries. He has directed many documentary and animation series on arts and history for young audiences. These series received different international awards and have been broadcast worldwide. Since 2004 he has worked in documentary projects with indigenous communities and communities in areas of armed conflict. In 2007 along with Diana Rico founded the production company 4direcciones audio-visual (www.4direcciones.tv) dedicated to the production of documentaries, feature length films, series, that celebrate cultural diversity and its close bond with biodiversity. Since 2008 it is an ally in the audiovisual processes of the GAIA Amazonas Foundation (www.gaiaamazonas.org) and in this work he participated by producing audiovisual pieces for the legal process to constitute the National Natural Park Yaigogé Apaporis, to protect an area of 1 million 100 thousand hectares of Colombian Amazon forest from mining. In 2009 he produced and co-directed the feature length documentary “The Sand and the Rain” along with Diana Rico and British director Peter Webber (Oscar Nominee for “Girl with a Pearl Earing”) for the public TV of Qatar. He has co-produced and directed three series for children. The series Omm-Moo received the international Pitch Award in Mipjunior at Cannes and has been broadcast by important TV stations in over 13 countries, among them Globo TV in Brazil and Sun TV in India. He has produced and done camera work for the movie Tawaii by Bruce Parry (Tribe of the BBC), series for Natgeo, Animal Planet, Discovery Channel, History Channel and Netflix. Since 2012 the work of 4Direcciones has expanded into other platforms such as web, games, apps, VR 360 content. He currently works on the Itinerant Festival and digital content platform called “Osos de Anteojos” (pectacled Bears). A project developed with the support of the fund from Innpulsa Colombia for technological innovation in culture with important environmental organizations. (www.festivalosos.com)

He is an associate director and producer of the feature film “Pickpockets”, an original production for Netflix. Produced along with Five7Media (RCN Colombia) and Full-house of France. This project is currently in post-production and will be released in theaters and on the NETFLIX platform. Since 2015 he has worked on the development of VR 360 projects, in association with Swiss-Canadian DOP Nicolás Jolliet and DEEP INC. with communities from the Colombian Amazon. In 2016 the Museum of Art of the National University of Colombia commissioned 4Direcciones for the sound installation and feature length documentary “The Origin of the Night”. He directed and produced this project with an interdisciplinary and multi-ethnic team of artists and researchers..In recent years Richard Décaillet’s audiovisual work has expanded beyond traditional screens of TV, Cinema, and Web, into spaces such as radio, museums, galleries and festivals. Integrating digital experiences with live events and expanding narratives, seeking to create an experience that inspires the audience to abandon passive consumption and to take action.

4DIRECCIONES Audio-Visual

History

4Direcciones is a Colombian audiovisual production company, internationally recognized for bringing environmental, indigenous, cultural, and young people's content to a variety of platforms.

4Direcciones has experience in feature films, series, and art installations that combine animation, documentary, and fiction, as well as live events reaching viewers in Latin America, Canada, the United States, Asia, and Europe.

Diana Rico and Richard Décaillet, a collaborative duo of artist-filmmakers, founded the company to explore the interconnections between art, spirituality, and territory.

Over the past ten years, 4Direcciones has become a space for dialogue between the ancestral practices of the native peoples of America and those of the urban world, uniting knowledge, trades, and tools to renew our relationship with the planet.

The high production values used in our projects, our commitment to the issues we address, and our respect for the communities and individuals with whom we work is what sets us apart.

We believe audiovisual content is a powerful instrument for social change, a tool for decolonization of the mind, a weapon for healing and liberation, and an altar of inspiration.

(www.4direcciones.tv)

Projects

4Direcciones was founded in 2007 after winning a call-for-entries from Colombia's SENA (National Training Service) and COLCIENCIAS (Department of Science, Technology, and Innovation) to promote cultural businesses focused on technological innovation.

In 2011, the company won the Bavaria Foundation's "Destapa Futuro" contest for business expansion and, for the second time, the SENA-COLCIENCIAS call-for-entries promoting the creation of animation studios.

In 2012, 4Direcciones was selected and backed by the Massachusetts Institute of Technology's (MIT) G-LAB to help introduce the company's content into the North American market.

4Direcciones's initial productions (2006-2008) focused on education and included the outstanding 10-episode series “Plástica: Arte Contemporáneo en Colombia", ("Contemporary Art in Colombia”), "Los colombianos tal como somos" ("Colombians: Just the Way We Are"), the documentary feature "Bogotá imaginada" (“Bogotá Imagined"), and the 12-episode series "Lado B de la historia" (“History’s B-Side”), chosen to represent Colombia at INPUT 2009 in Poland as Best Colombian Cultural Program. This initial content is now used as important educational tools in the programs' corresponding fields in Colombia.

Since its inception, 4Directions has participated in the audiovisual processes of indigenous peoples involved in the GAIA Amazonas Foundation (www.gaiaamazonas.com), including the legal work required to found the Yaigogé Apaporis National Park and preserve 1.1 million hectares of the Colombian Amazon from the effects of mining.

As part of this alliance, 4D produced and directed the audiovisual projects "Encuentros sagrados" ("Sacred Encounters") (2007), "Guardianes de la libertad" ("Guardians of Freedom") (2008), and "Los jaguares de Yuruparí" ("The Jaguars of Yuruparí") (2010) and provided production services for the NatGeo program directed by Wade Davis, " Light at the Edge of the World: Amazon", filmed in the Pira Paraná River communities of Vaupés.

In 2009-2010, Diana and Richard produced and co-directed a three-part documentary series with Peter Webber ("The Girl with the Pearl Earring" and "Emperor") for Qatar TV about the meeting of a group of Qataris in Colombia's Vaupés region and a group of shamans in the Qatar desert. "The Sand and the Rain" premiered in Doha in 2010 as part of the National Qatar Day celebrations, opened the Aljazeera Documentary Festival, and screened during 2010 as part of Qatar Airways inflight entertainment.

In 2010, 4D founded an animation studio and premiered the animated short film "Omm Moo yoga para niños" (based on the children's book by María Villegas and Jennie Kent published by Villegas Editores) in more than 100 cinemas throughout Colombia. This project led to the co-production (with Chile and Argentina) of a 26-episode animated series that premiered in 2013 and continues to air in Colombia, Chile, Argentina, Brazil, Ecuador, Panama, Mexico, the United States, and Portugal, and on southern India's Sun TV channel.

"Omm Moo Yoga for Kids" won the MIP Junior International Competition at the 2012 Cannes Film Festival and, as a result, was widely distributed in Latin America, Europe, and the US.

The 4D animation studio developed, produced, and directed the in-house series "Figúrate" and "Eco S.O.S.", which premiered in several countries during 2013 and 2014.

In 2013, Parque Explora of Medellín commissioned 4D to develop part of the audiovisual content for the Bogotá District Planetarium.

In 2014, 4D designed and were the supervising producers in Colombia of the feature documentary "TAWAII" directed by Bruce Parry (BBC's "Tribe") produced by Damian Hirst and Julian Lennon; designed and produced the Chilean series "Hijos de las estrellas" ("Children of the Stars") about astronomy in America; and co-produced with Mexico and Spain the animation for the documentary "El ruiseñor y la noche" (“The Nightingale and the Night”) about Chavela Vargas's relationship with poet Federico García Lorca.

In 2015, 4D worked on a series of documentary shorts about the gastronomy of Colombia's Magdalena province. The shorts are part of an audiovisual installation at the Banco de la República's new Gold Museum in the city of Santa Marta. They were also involved in several web activism campaigns focused on preservation of water, the highland páramos, jungles, and against human trafficking.

This same year, the 4D documentary "Los jaguares del Yuruparí" ("The Jaguars of Yuruparí") screened in more than 120 cinemas in principal cities around Colombia to celebrate the inclusion of the knowledge of the Amazonian Pirá Paraná River communities on the UN's Intangible Heritage of Humanity list.

4D acquired the rights to Wade Davis' novel "The River" and in 2016 began developing scripts to produce multiplatform content based on the book.

In 2015-2016, 4D provided production services to the Netflix "Behind-the-scenes on Narcos" series; research and production for several episodes of the History Channel's "Hunting Hitler" series; local production for the Travel Channel's "Booze Traveler" series; and production services for NatGeo Canada's documentary, "Facing Pablo".

In 2016, the Art Museum of the National University of Colombia commissioned 4D to create the sound and documentary feature installation "El origen de la noche" ("The Origin of the Night"), which was exhibited at the museum from September to April 2017 and drew an average of 600 visitors daily. This sound and video installation will travel to museums throughout the US and Europe in 2017-2018.

Also in 2016, 4Direcciones produced its first fiction feature, "Pick-Pockets" for Netflix. The project received major attention at audiovisual markets and meetings, including the Producers' Encounters at the Cartagena International Film Festival, the Berlinale, and the Bogotá Audiovisual Market.

Associate directors Diana Rico and Richard Décaillet teamed up on this project with British director Peter Webber and executive producers Five7Media and France's Full-House. The project is currently in post-production and will be released in theaters and on the Netflix platform in late 2017.

Since 2015, 4Direcciones has developed virtual reality (VR) projects in alliance with Swiss-Canadian filmmaker Nicolás Jolliet, and with communities in the Colombian Amazon. These alliances led to the VR fiction short "WIRATI", a co-production with Canada's Deep Inc. (www.deep-inc.com), currently in the development and funding stages.

4D also runs the 7Flechas Foundation, which spearheads the children's environmental Osos de Anteojos (Spectacled Bear) Festival, awarded a development grant from INNPULSA COLOMBIA's innovation program in 2015. The festival, an annual event produced in collaboration with environmental organizations such as the OPEPA, Green Peace Colombia, Avaaz, Slow Food, and the Rural Hub, brings children and young people into contact with music, art, technology, ancestral practices, and preservation of the Andean páramo (highland) ecosystem.

(www.festivalosos.com)
